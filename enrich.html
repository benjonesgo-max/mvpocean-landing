Why Enrich

The hours between 3 and 5 shouldn't be a waiting room.

Most after-school care is a gym floor and a bin of old toys — kids kill time until pickup. Those ten hours a week could be swim strokes, piano chords, and paint under fingernails instead. Enrich turns dead time into the richest part of a kid's day, and because we go to the community instead of renting a building, every dollar goes into the program — not the rent.

01

A real curriculum, not babysitting

Every day has a theme and a destination — actual instructors, actual skills, actual progress kids are proud of.

02

The whole town is our classroom

Pools, rinks, studios, libraries, trails, community centres — venues that sit half-empty from 3 to 5 become our campus. No rent, no walls.

03

Shared ride, shared cost

One small crew of kids from one or two schools shares a caregiver, a van, and the cost — private-nanny quality at a fraction of the price.

The napkin math

Nanny-level attention. Group-level price.

Hiring someone privately for the 3-to-5 gap runs about $1,000 a month per family. Share the caregiver, the van, and the adventures across a small crew, and the same enriched two hours costs a fraction — with more fun, because friends came along.

Built on trust

Boringly serious about the safety part.

The fun only works if the foundation is airtight. Enrich is being designed from day one around BC's child-care licensing and passenger-transport rules — not around loopholes.

🪪 Licensed school-age program 🚐 Class 4 licensed drivers 👥 Staffed ratios on every ride ✅ Criminal record checks ⛑️ First-aid certified 📍 Live pickup & drop-off alerts
